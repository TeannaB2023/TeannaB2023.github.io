Introductory Statement Essays Making Connections


Author's Statement

I’ve been fed up with “the White man.” The inconsiderate, oppressive, and discriminatory “White man” that seems to be orchestrating the social destruction of communities I care about or belong to. I’m upset because “he” has made our world so dark and depressing that it’s difficult to have hope. However, in these pieces of writing, I want to challenge the audience to view despair as a catalyst. Despair can incite a chain reaction that rallies, empowers, and legitimizes movements of rebellion against “the man.” Although every piece doesn’t have a triumphant ending or a clear solution in sight, the discussion of the ideas, organizations, and individuals that are pushing against the depressing status quo can engender hope. After writing these pieces, I realize that hope isn’t world-changing because it is clear-cut. Rather, hope is amazing because it’s limitless. No one (not even “the White man”) can control hope because it belongs to the soul it occupies.
